title: "AIxBio: First Impressions"
date: 2026-02-02
description: Early thoughts on where machine learning is genuinely moving the needle in biology.
---
Machine learning has moved from a niche tool to a core part of the modern
biology toolkit. A few areas where I've seen the most genuine progress:

- **Protein structure and design** — models that go beyond prediction into
  generative design of novel folds and functions.
- **Sequence-to-function models** — learning regulatory grammar directly
  from large-scale sequencing data.
- **Lab automation feedback loops** — closing the loop between model
  predictions and wet-lab validation faster than traditional design-build-test
  cycles.

I'll be posting deeper dives on each of these as I explore them further.
